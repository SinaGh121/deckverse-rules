Don Club Scenarios

Speaking order and voting process - Version 1.0

Speaking Order in Don Club Scenarios

  • Day 1: Start from player 1 and go clockwise up to player 10.
  • Day 2: Start from player 10 and go counter-clockwise down to player 1.
  • Day 3: Start from player 5 up to player 10, then from player 1 to player 4, clockwise from the middle of the deck.
  • Day 4: Start from player 6 down to player 1, then from player 10 to player 7, counter-clockwise from the middle of the deck.
  • Day 5: Same as day 1, then the five-day cycle repeats.

This sequence is shown for a 10-player deck; keep the same pattern for other counts (e.g., with 12 players, day 2 starts from player 6).

Voting Process in Don Club Scenarios

Minimum votes for defense or elimination

  • 13 to 11 players: 5 votes for defense and 5 votes for elimination.
  • 10 to 8 players: 4 votes for defense and 4 votes for elimination.
  • 7 or 6 players: 3 votes for defense and 3 votes for elimination.
  • 5 or 4 players: 2 votes for defense and 2 votes for elimination.

Vote difference and single defense

  • If the difference between two players reaches 3 votes or more, only the player with more votes goes to defense (single defense).
  • If the difference is less than 3 votes, both players go to defense.

Record (Sabegheh)

  • Every time a player goes to defense and survives, they gain one record point.
  • If two players in defense receive the same number of elimination votes, the one with more record points is eliminated, even if the difference is only one.
  • Being a target for the Capo gun or being interrogated does not count toward record points.

Voting rights inside defense

  • If there are only two players in defense, they are not allowed to vote for each other.
  • If there are three or more players in defense, they may vote for each other.

Target, Cover and "About"

  • If there is a single player in defense, they may choose two speakers: one against them (target) and one supporting them (cover).
  • If there are two players in defense, each of them may choose only one person to speak about them; there is no separate target and cover in this case.
  • If there are three or more players in defense, none of them may choose anyone to speak about themselves; in this situation there is no target, no cover and no "about" for themselves.
