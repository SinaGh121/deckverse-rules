Mafia - Bounty on the Boss

Advanced Mafia scenario guide - Version 1.0

Scenario Overview

This is the “Bounty on the Boss” variant (common in advanced Mafia). Two key roles create instant win conditions if they are eliminated by daytime voting.

Quick Summary

  • If the Godfather is eliminated by daytime vote, the Mafia immediately lose.
  • If the Doctor is eliminated by daytime vote, the Town immediately lose.
  • All other win conditions follow standard advanced Mafia rules.

Suggested Roles (15 players)

Mafia Team (5)

  • Godfather (Mafia boss)
  • Terrorist
  • Natasha (Silencer)
  • Lawyer
  • Vanilla Mafia

Town Team (10)

  • Doctor
  • Judge
  • Detective
  • Priest
  • Sniper
  • Bodyguard
  • 4 Vanilla Towns

Declaring “vanilla town” is allowed in this scenario.

Win Conditions

Standard

  • Town win: all Mafia are eliminated.
  • Mafia win: Mafia count becomes equal to town count.

Bounty Conditions

  • If the Godfather is voted out during the day: instant town win.
  • If the Doctor is voted out during the day: instant Mafia win.

Introduction Night (Night 0)

The moderator introduces roles so teams learn teammates.

Mafia Intro

  • Mafia wake together and learn each other.
  • Godfather makes the night shot decision with team input.

Godfather Swap (Night 0 only)

  • Godfather may once, and only on Night 0, swap their role with another Mafia member.
  • They give Godfather to someone else and take that player’s Mafia role.

Town Intro

  • Key town power roles are introduced (Detective, Doctor, Judge, Sniper, Bodyguard, Priest).
  • In this scenario, the Judge sees the Doctor’s and Godfather’s likes together on Night 0 but cannot tell which is which.

Roles & Rules

Godfather (Mafia)

  • Decides the night shot (with Mafia consultation).
  • Detective check on Godfather is negative.
  • If Sniper shoots Godfather, Godfather dies but this does not trigger the bounty win; bounty only triggers if voted out by day.

Terrorist (Mafia)

  • Daytime suicide role.
  • May declare terror only during the day and before voting starts.
  • After moderator confirms, chooses one player to die with them.
  • If Sniper kills Terrorist at night, terror does not trigger.
  • If Terrorist is voted out, terror does not trigger.

Natasha (Silencer, Mafia)

  • Silences one player each night.
  • Silenced player cannot speak, vote, or react the next day.
  • Cannot silence the same player on consecutive nights.

Lawyer (Mafia)

  • Selects one player nightly.
  • That player’s Detective result is inverted for that night.
  • Can invert Godfather’s result as well.

Vanilla Mafia

  • No special ability.
  • Wakes at night to know and consult with the team.

Priest (Town)

  • Counter to Natasha.
  • Each night has two picks:
    • First: “unsilence” target.
    • Second: grant “extra speech” to target.
  • If Priest and Natasha pick the same target in one night, there is no silence next day.
  • Priest’s second target gets extra speaking time next day and often starts the day’s talking (per moderator).

Detective (Town)

  • Checks one player each night.
  • Results in this scenario:
    • Godfather: negative
    • Towns: negative
    • Other Mafia: positive
  • Must watch for Lawyer because results can be inverted.

Sniper (Town)

  • Limited shots (announced by moderator).
  • May shoot each night.
  • If Sniper kills a town, there is no fireback; the citizen dies.
  • If Sniper kills Terrorist at night, terror does not trigger.

Bodyguard (Town)

  • Counter to Terrorist.
  • When Terrorist declares and names a target, Bodyguard has a short window (e.g., 5 seconds) to stand in.
  • If on time: Bodyguard and Terrorist both die; target survives.
  • While Terrorist is alive, Bodyguard is night-immune to Mafia shots (cannot be killed at night by Mafia).

Doctor (Town)

  • Each night may save one player from the Mafia shot.
  • Self-save allowed.
  • Number of effective saves depends on player count (common: two saves above a threshold, single save below it).

Judge (Town)

  • Once per game may return a player who was voted out.
  • Suggested sequence:
    • Voted-out player gives a short will.
    • Everyone sleeps; Judge wakes alone to decide to return or confirm elimination.
  • After first use, Judge becomes Vanilla Town.
  • Scenario note: on Night 0, Judge sees two “likes” belonging to Doctor and Godfather but cannot distinguish them.

Vanilla Town

  • No special ability.
  • Declaring vanilla town is allowed in this scenario.

Suggested Night Order (Moderator)

Keep a fixed order to avoid clashes:

  1. Mafia wake and consult.
  2. Godfather decides the night shot.
  3. Natasha (silence).
  4. Lawyer (invert check).
  5. Doctor (save).
  6. Detective (check).
  7. Priest (unsilence and extra speech).
  8. Sniper (if shooting).
  9. All sleep.

Suggested Day Flow

  1. Announce night results (deaths, saves, silences, extra speech per rules).
  2. Talks and defenses.
  3. Terrorist declaration (if desired) before voting.
  4. Voting.
  5. If someone is voted:
    • Will (short speech) then Judge decision (if using).
  6. Check win conditions (standard and bounty).

Key Execution Notes

  • Bounty triggers only on daytime votes, not night deaths.
  • Clarify timers before play:
    • Bodyguard reaction window vs. Terrorist.
    • Extra speech duration.
    • Defense and voting rules.
  • Moderator should announce clearly:
    • Sniper bullet count.
    • Doctor’s two-save vs. one-save threshold.
    • Priest’s extra speech timing and order.
