Shahname Mafia Scenario

A story of Ahriman against the Light

Shahname Scenario Overview

In the Shahname scenario, players are divided into two main sides: the Ahrimans, equivalent to the Mafia team, and the Light side, equivalent to the citizens.

A major part of the game depends on managing the feathers of Simorgh and Bof, abilities being taken by Zahhak, and the chain of guesses and decisions between Afrasiyab, Rostam, and Arash.

This scenario has no introduction day. After roles are dealt, the game goes directly into introduction night so the Ahriman members can identify each other and the game can begin.

Teams

  • Ahrimans: Zahhak, Bof, and Afrasiyab.
  • Light side: two simple allies and five special roles.

Important Feather Rule

The moderator does not reveal the feather type and only says: "You have a feather." Simorgh's feather has no effect against Bof's poisoned feather, and keeping the poisoned feather removes the player from the game.

Night Order

  • Ahriman team: the Ahrimans wake up. Zahhak chooses the night target, or if Bof is using the feather, the team chooses Bof's feather target instead of the night target. Zahhak may also choose an ability-taking target if still available.
  • Simorgh: chooses how to assign feathers.
  • Kaveh: may save a citizen whose ability was taken by Zahhak.
  • Jamasb: performs a normal check or the one-time blood check.
  • Rostam: if his shadow has not been used, chooses one player for the shadow.
  • Arash the Archer: if he has not given away the bow and arrow, gives them to one member of the Light side.
  • Feathers are announced before morning. The moderator does not reveal the feather type and only says: "You have a feather."

Voting

  • With 8, 9, or 10 players, the threshold to enter defense is 4 votes and the threshold for elimination is also 4 votes.
  • With 6 or 7 players, the threshold to enter defense is 3 votes and the threshold for elimination is also 3 votes.
  • With 4 or 5 players, the threshold to enter defense is 2 votes and the threshold for elimination is also 2 votes.

Roles

Ahriman Roles

Zahhak

Leader of the Ahrimans and the night shot caller.

  • Issues the Ahriman team's shot order each night.
  • During the game, may take a citizen's ability up to two times.
  • Can use the ability-taking power only once per night.
  • Kaveh can save a citizen who has been targeted by Zahhak's ability-taking action.

Tags: team leader, 2 ability takes.

Bof

Ahriman ally and holder of the poisoned feather.

  • Has only one poisoned feather during the whole game.
  • At night, the Ahriman team must choose either the night shot or Bof's feather. They cannot do both in the same night.
  • When a feather reaches a player, the moderator says "You have a feather" without revealing its type, and the player decides whether to keep it.
  • If the player keeps the poisoned feather, that player leaves the game.
  • Simorgh's feather does not cancel Bof's feather.

Tags: once per game, death priority.

Afrasiyab

The Ahriman enchanter during the midday sleep.

  • In the conflict involving Rostam's shadow, wakes before Rostam's final decision and guesses his identity.
  • In Arash's arrow conflict, also wakes first and tries to identify Arash.
  • A correct guess on Rostam enchants him and removes Rostam's immortality.
  • If he correctly guesses Arash, only Arash's shield ability is lost. Afrasiyab does not take the arrow, and the arrow remains under the control of its holder.
  • Afrasiyab correctly appears positive to Jamasb's normal check.

Tags: Rostam guess, Arash guess.

Light Roles

Light Allies

Two citizens with no night ability.

  • Two players are present as Light Allies.
  • They have no special power and must find the Ahrimans through discussion, voting, and cooperation with the Light roles.

Tags: 2 players, no night action.

Simorgh

Guardian of the Light and holder of five feathers.

  • Has five feathers in total during the game.
  • Can keep only one feather for themself; the other four feathers are for other citizens.
  • When a feather reaches a player, the moderator does not reveal its type and only says: "You have a feather."
  • A player who keeps Simorgh's feather stays in the game if targeted by the night shot.
  • Simorgh's feather has no effect against Bof's poisoned feather.

Tags: 5 feathers, save from shot.

Jamasb

Investigator with misleading results.

  • In a normal check, Afrasiyab and Bof correctly return positive.
  • Simorgh incorrectly returns positive despite being on the Light side.
  • Once per game, Jamasb may perform a blood check: asks the moderator about one player, and receives a positive answer if that player is Rostam or Zahhak.
  • Simorgh returning positive in a normal check does not mean Simorgh belongs to the Ahriman team.

Tags: false-positive Simorgh, one blood check.

Kaveh

Liberator of citizens from Zahhak's bite.

  • During the game, may save a citizen whose ability has been taken by Zahhak, up to two times.
  • If Zahhak is still in the game and Kaveh leaves the game by the night shot, the Ahrimans have no shot on the following night.

Tags: 2 saves, next-night shot stop.

Rostam

Immortal shadow hero with one decision opportunity.

  • Rostam is immortal and once per game places his shadow on one player.
  • The next day, the moderator announces that a shadow exists, and at the end of the conflict, midday sleep begins.
  • Afrasiyab first guesses Rostam. A correct guess enchants Rostam and makes him mortal.
  • If the guess is wrong, Rostam decides whether the player under the shadow leaves the game or stays.
  • If the removed player is Light, Rostam becomes mortal.
  • If Rostam hits a Light player at night, he also becomes mortal.
  • If the shadow target leaves due to a poisoned feather, the shadow disappears.
  • Rostam's day action has no effect on Zahhak. Zahhak does not leave, and Rostam identifies him.

Tags: immortal, one shadow.

Arash the Archer

Holder of the arrow, bow, and shield decision.

  • One night, gives his bow and arrow to one member of the Light side.
  • The arrow holder may announce the arrow and name one target at any time on the following day.
  • The game enters night and Afrasiyab first tries to guess Arash.
  • If Afrasiyab guesses Arash correctly, only Arash's shield ability is lost and the arrow is not taken from its holder.
  • If Afrasiyab's guess is wrong, Arash wakes and may give a shield to one of the players who entered defense.
  • Arash can never take the arrow back or take the final shot authority from the arrow holder.
  • After day returns, the target defends, and the arrow holder makes the final decision to shoot or cancel.
  • If Arash himself is in defense, he cannot give a shield to himself.

Tags: one bow and arrow, shield option.

Key Rules and Ability Interactions

  1. Starts with introduction night: after roles are dealt, no introduction day is held and the game begins directly with introduction night.
  2. Voting with 8 to 10 players: the threshold to enter defense is 4 votes and the threshold for elimination is also 4 votes.
  3. Voting with 6 or 7 players: the threshold to enter defense is 3 votes and the threshold for elimination is also 3 votes.
  4. Voting with 4 or 5 players: the threshold to enter defense is 2 votes and the threshold for elimination is also 2 votes.
  5. Feather announcement and keeping: the moderator only says "You have a feather." Keeping Simorgh's feather saves from the night shot, while keeping the poisoned feather removes the player from the game.
  6. Kaveh shot consequence: if Zahhak is alive and Kaveh leaves by the night shot, the Ahriman team cannot shoot on the following night.
  7. Rostam's enchantment: Afrasiyab's correct guess removes Rostam's immortality and makes him mortal for the rest of the game.
  8. Rostam's mistake: removing a Light player by Rostam's decision, or Rostam hitting a Light player at night, makes Rostam mortal.
  9. Rostam against Zahhak: Rostam's day action does not remove Zahhak. Instead, Rostam learns that the target is Zahhak.
  10. Arash's shield limit: Arash may give a shield only to one player who has entered defense. He does not take the arrow back, and if he is in defense himself, he cannot give a shield to himself.
  11. Shot or Bof feather: each night, the Ahriman team either shoots or uses Bof's feather; doing both at the same time is not allowed.
  12. Two feathers reaching one player: if Bof's feather and Simorgh's feather both reach the same player, the moderator touches that player's hand only once. If the player keeps the feather, they leave the game.

Special Conflict Flow

  1. Rostam's shadow: Rostam chooses one player, and the next day the moderator announces that a shadow exists on that player.
  2. Rostam's midday sleep: at the end of the conflict, Afrasiyab guesses Rostam. Then the enchantment result is announced to Rostam, and if not enchanted, he decides whether the target leaves.
  3. Arash's arrow announcement: the arrow holder names the target during the day, and the game enters night to run the guess phase.
  4. Arash's midday sleep: Afrasiyab guesses Arash. A correct guess only removes Arash's shield ability; if wrong, Arash may give a shield to one player in defense.
  5. Final archer decision: after the target defends during the day, the arrow holder chooses whether to shoot or cancel.

Shahname FAQ

When does Simorgh's feather save a player?

The moderator says "You have a feather" without revealing the type. If the feather is Simorgh's, the player keeps it, and that player is targeted by the night shot, the player stays in the game.

How many times can Zahhak take citizens' abilities?

At most two times in the whole game, and only once per night.

What removes Rostam's immortality?

Afrasiyab's correct guess, mistakenly removing a Light player by Rostam's decision, or Rostam hitting a Light player at night.

What happens if Afrasiyab correctly guesses Arash?

Only Arash's shield ability is lost. The arrow is not taken from its holder, and the final shot decision remains with the arrow holder.

Which roles return positive to Jamasb?

In a normal check, Afrasiyab and Bof correctly return positive, and Simorgh falsely returns positive. In the blood check, the answer is positive for Rostam or Zahhak.
