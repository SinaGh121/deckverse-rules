Werewolf Scenario

Version 1.9

Number of Players

  • Ideal range: 8 to 13 players.
  • Recommended ratio: 1 werewolf for every 3 villagers.

Teams & Moderation

  • Two teams: Villagers and Werewolves.
  • The game is run by the Moderator.

Win Conditions

  • Werewolves: when the number of living werewolves is greater than or equal to the number of living villagers.
  • Villagers: when all werewolves are eliminated.
  • Handshake/Chaos phase: if at the start of a day only 3 players remain:
    • 2 minutes of open discussion.
    • Each player must pick one player as a Villager and shake hands with them.
    • If the two Villagers shake hands, the Village wins; otherwise, the Werewolves win.

Introduction Day

  • Everyone only knows their own role; the werewolves do not yet know their teammates.
  • Turn-based talking, max 1 minute per player.
  • No challenges and no voting.

Introduction Night

  • The Moderator wakes the werewolf team so they learn their teammates.

Days

  • Turn-based talking, max 1 minute per player.
  • Others may signal to react by raising a hand.
  • Any player may, by raising a hand, request a challenge from the current speaker and speak out of turn for 30 seconds.

Voting

Stage 1: Nomination for Defense

  • Each player may nominate multiple people.
  • Anyone receiving at least half the votes, rounded up, goes to defense. Example: with 5 or 6 players, 3 votes are required.

Stage 2: Defense

  • Each nominee defends for 1 minute.
  • During a defense, any reactions (like/dislike, etc.) are forbidden; violation removes your voting right for that day.

Stage 3: Elimination Vote

  • If only one person is in defense: they are eliminated with at least half the votes, rounded up.
  • If multiple people are in defense: the highest vote-getter is eliminated.
  • If there is a tie between two or more players: no one is eliminated.
  • A tie results in no elimination.
  • Nominees in defense still have voting rights.

Will

A player eliminated by majority vote may give a short will (up to 1 minute). Role disclosure remains forbidden.

Nights

Wake Order

  • Woodsman — if eliminated by vote during the previous day (to choose a companion).
  • Werewolves
  • Guard
  • Seer
  • Resurrector
  • Alchemist
  • Woodsman — if killed by werewolves this night (to choose a companion).

Butchery

  • Each night, instead of the usual attack or a conversion, the werewolves may guess a player’s exact role and butcher them.
  • If the guess is correct, the player is immediately eliminated; if wrong, the werewolves’ night action is spent and no death occurs from them.
  • The check is against the player’s initial role card; if someone was turned into a Vanilla Villager during the game, the initial role still applies.
  • The presence or absence of Alpha does not matter; the remaining werewolves may agree collectively to perform a butchery.
  • If a butchery happens, the Moderator announces in the morning that the player was “butchered.”

Woodsman

  • When the Woodsman leaves the game, the Moderator announces it; the Woodsman immediately names one player as a companion to take down with them, and the Moderator announces that name.
  • The result is immediate; if successful, the companion is eliminated at once.

Alchemist

  • After each night and before eyes open, the Moderator asks everyone to extend one hand. If a player has been converted into a werewolf by the Alchemist’s ability, the Moderator touches that player’s hand.
  • The converted player does not know the other werewolves until the next night, and the werewolves are unaware of their joining until then.

Status Checks

  • At the start of any day, players may ask the Moderator to announce how many villagers and how many werewolves have been eliminated so far.
  • In 9 to 13 player games, at most 2 checks are allowed. (The number of checks scales with player count.)

No Role Disclosure

  • Revealing roles or night abilities is strictly forbidden (directly or indirectly). Violation leads to elimination at the start of the next day.
  • You may only reference Vanilla Villager, Werewolves, or people outside the game.
  • The Seer may not make a definitive claim that someone is a werewolf based solely on a positive check without daytime reasoning. If this rule is broken, the Seer is eliminated at the start of the next day.

Roles

Werewolves

Alpha

  • Has a shield that protects against the Woodsman’s attack or the Alchemist’s conversion.
  • Appears as Villager to the Seer.

Black Wolf

  • Can convert a Vanilla Villager into a Werewolf.
  • Only Vanilla Villagers can be converted; special roles like Seer, Woodsman, and Guard cannot be converted.
  • The converted player opens eyes with the werewolves that same night and joins their team.

Wizard

  • Each night targets one player and disables or inverts their ability for that night and the following day.
  • The effect lasts only for that night and the next day, then expires.

Examples:

  • Guard: after being bewitched, cannot protect anyone.
  • Seer: if bewitched, the check result is inverted.
  • Woodsman: if killed while bewitched, their attack is nullified.

Werewolf

  • Players who become werewolves during the game receive this role.
  • Each night, opens eyes with the other werewolves and participates in night decisions.

Villagers

Villager

  • The baseline role with no night ability; relies on discussion, reasoning, and daytime voting.

Guard

  • May protect one player each night.
  • Protection works against all night attacks except a correct Butchery, daytime elimination, and Black Wolf conversion on a Vanilla Villager.
  • Cannot protect themself or the same player on consecutive nights.
  • If bewitched, their ability is ineffective that night and no one is protected.

Seer

  • Each night checks one player and receives the result as Villager or Werewolf.
  • The Moderator reports the result via hand gesture: thumbs up means Werewolf, thumbs down means Villager.
  • If bewitched, the result is inverted.
  • Checking Alpha always reports Villager.
  • Checking Scarecrow always reports Werewolf.

Resurrector

  • May revive one eliminated player once per game.
  • Reviving a butchered player or the Woodsman is not allowed.
  • If bewitched on the night they intend to revive, no revival happens and they become a Vanilla Villager.
  • After the first use, their ability is lost and they become a Vanilla Villager.

Alchemist

  • Acts only once during the whole game; afterwards becomes a Vanilla Villager.
  • At night chooses a target; if the target is a Werewolf, they die. If the target is a Vanilla Villager who started the game as a Vanilla Villager, they are converted into a Werewolf.
  • Has no effect on powered villagers, and the ability is consumed.
  • Alpha with an active shield and any target protected by the Guard that night are immune.
  • If bewitched that same night, no action occurs and the ability is lost.

Woodsman

  • If killed at night by the werewolves, at the end of that night they may eliminate one player. If eliminated by vote during the day, at the start of the next night they may eliminate one player.
  • If butchered, they have no right to eliminate; if bewitched that day, their action is nullified.
  • Invalid targets for the Woodsman: Alpha with an active shield, Scarecrow with an active shield, and any player protected by the Guard.

Red Riding Hood

  • While the Woodsman is alive, is immune to the werewolves’ night attack.
  • When the Woodsman dies, this immunity is lost and Red Riding Hood becomes a Vanilla Villager.

Scarecrow

  • If put on defense, must remain completely silent; otherwise is eliminated immediately.
  • The Seer’s check on Scarecrow always reports Werewolf.
  • Has a one-time shield that protects against one werewolf attack or one Woodsman attack, then it is consumed.

Canary

  • The Canary card is not in the main deck; during Introduction Night, the Moderator secretly designates one Vanilla Villager as the Canary. The Canary is unaware and is introduced only to the werewolves.
  • If the Canary leaves the game in any way, on the next night the werewolves have no action: no attack, no butchery, no conversion. This suspension is not publicly announced.
  • The Canary cannot be converted and is considered a powered role (not a Vanilla Villager) for rules purposes.

Role Setup

Note: In 8 and 9 player scenarios, Black Wolf is absent; however, both werewolves have the Black Wolf "conversion" ability.
