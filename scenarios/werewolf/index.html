Overview

Werewolf is a social deduction game. The village tries to identify and eliminate the Werewolves by voting during the day, while the Werewolves secretly eliminate a player each night.

Players: 6–20 • Time: 10–45 min • Teams: Village vs. Werewolves (plus optional third-party roles)

Setup

  1. Gather players in a circle and choose a Moderator (or use the DeckVerse app).
  2. Select a role set based on player count (see templates below).
  3. Shuffle roles and deal one face-down to each player. Players keep roles secret.
  4. Moderator explains win conditions and timing, then starts the first Night.

Phases

Night

  1. Moderator: “Everyone close your eyes.”
  2. Werewolves open eyes and silently pick a target.
  3. Doctor selects someone to protect.
  4. Seer checks one player’s alignment.
  5. Other special roles act as applicable.
  6. Moderator: “Night ends.”

Day

  1. Moderator announces if anyone was eliminated overnight.
  2. Open discussion. Players may accuse, defend, and share suspicions.
  3. Lynch vote: Use majority voting. Optional: timed discussion (e.g., 3–5 minutes).
  4. Eliminated players reveal role and leave the circle (no table talk).

Voting & timing

  • Use simple majority to eliminate during day. On ties, revote; if tied again, no one is eliminated.
  • Recommended round pacing: Night: 30–60s, Day: 3–5min, Vote: 30s.
  • Silent/thumbs voting helps large groups. Use the DeckVerse timer if available.

Endgame & win conditions

  • Village wins when all Werewolves are eliminated.
  • Werewolves win when they are equal to or outnumber the remaining players.
  • Some optional roles (e.g., Tanner) have unique win conditions.

Variants & house rules

  • No first-night kill: First night ends without a kill to ease in new players.
  • Reveal/no-reveal: Decide whether eliminated players reveal roles.
  • Secret Doctor: Doctor cannot self-protect twice in a row (optional).
  • Seer cadence: Seer acts every night or odd nights only.
  • Open roles list: Reveal which roles are in the game, but not who has them.

FAQ

Can players talk at night?

No. Only roles that act at night open their eyes. Everyone else remains silent.

What if the Werewolves and Doctor target the same player?

The target survives if protected that night.

Can eliminated players speak?

No. They leave the circle and may not influence the game.
